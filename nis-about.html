NIS SUMMARY

The Nigeria Immigration Service (NIS) is the government
agency that has been charged with the responsibility of
migration management in Nigeria.

Over the years, the NIS has witnessed series of changes since it was extracted
from the Nigeria Police Force (NPF) in 1958. The Immigration Department, as
it was known then, was extrusted with the core immigration duties under the
headship of the Chief Federal Immigration Officer (CFIO) and the first to sit on
that chair was in the person of Mr. EH Harrison. The department in its
emergent stages inherited the Immigration Ordinances of 1958 for its
operation. At inception, the department had a narrow operational scope and
maintained a low profile and simple approach in attaining the desired goes
and objectives of government. During this period, only the Visa and Business
Sections were set up.

Vision and Mission Statements:

Vision:

To be a modern, effective and efficient Immigration Service, manned by well
trained and motivated workforce.

Mission:

To strengthen the security and propriety of Nigeria through proactive
effective and efficient border security and migration management.
